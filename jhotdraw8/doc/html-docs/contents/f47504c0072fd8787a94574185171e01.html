Description

A "design decision" documents a choice for a specific design out of a selection of alternative designs.

A design decision can be an arbitrary choice or an informed choice.

  • An arbitrary design decision occurs when the alternatives all have the same quality (like the "road rule" decision example shown below). It does not matter which one we pick, yet the choice may affect many other design decisions.
  • An informed design decision occurs when the alternatives have different trade-offs.

Example for an arbritrary design decision for a "road traffic rule":

<<design decision>> Road Traffic Rule text=Vehicles drive on the right side of the street and give way to incoming traffic from the right (Alternative designs that we could have picked are "drive on left, give way to left", "drive on left, give way to right", "drive on right, give way to left", )

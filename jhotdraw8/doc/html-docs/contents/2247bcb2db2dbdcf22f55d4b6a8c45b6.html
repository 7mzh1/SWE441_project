design assumption

Description

A "design assumption" documents a decision based on an assumption about the requirements.

Typically, a design assumption is made to make a requirement more specific, so that the design can be made simpler. Design assumptions may break, because it may turn out, that the requirement was indeed meant to be more general. So it is important that we document them.

Example for a design assumption:

<<design assumption>> Placement of Road Traffic Signals text=The requirements do not state which Road Traffic Rules apply. We assume that vehicles drive on the right side of the street and give way to incoming traffic from the right. Therefore we assume that road traffic signals are placed on the right side of the streets.
